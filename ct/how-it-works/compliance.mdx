Fairblock's confidential transfers are designed for regulatory compliance from the ground up. Addresses stay transparent while amounts and balances are encrypted.

## Selective Disclosure

Reveal specific transaction details to authorized parties without exposing your complete financial history.

| Disclosure Type | What is revealed | Who can access |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Transaction audit** | Specific transaction amounts | Designated auditors |
| **Balance proof** | Proof of sufficient funds | Counterparties |
| **Full account** | Complete transaction history | Regulators with warrants |

## Compliance Framework Compatibility

| Framework | Status |
| --- | --- |
| **OFAC** | Compatible. Addresses are transparent for sanctions screening |
| **FinCEN** | Compatible. Selective disclosure supports SAR filing |
| **MiCA** | Compatible. Built-in audit capabilities |
| **Travel Rule** | Compatible. Sender and receiver addresses are public |

## How It Works

Decryption keys are generated via distributed key generation (DKG), per-account and per-auditor. Auditors receive purpose-limited keys that decrypt only the data covered by their authorization.

<Note>
  Fairblock does not provide all-or-nothing privacy. The system is designed so that the right parties can see the right data, with cryptographic guarantees.
</Note>
