## The Problem

Public blockchains expose every financial detail. Payment amounts, wallet balances, transaction timing, and trading strategies are all visible to anyone watching.

This creates real-world risks:

- **Competitive intelligence** — rivals and adversaries track treasury movements
- **Front-running** — MEV bots and market makers exploit visible order flow
- **Whale targeting** — high-value wallets become social engineering and theft targets
- **Compliance friction** — full transparency conflicts with financial privacy regulations

## Fairblock's Approach

Fairblock implements **confidential by default** finance: amounts and balances are encrypted while addresses stay transparent.

This mirrors traditional finance — amounts are private, identities are known — enabling AML screening, sanctions compliance, and selective disclosure to auditors.

## Key Advantages

| Feature | Fairblock | Traditional Privacy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Interoperability** | Works across existing chains without bridges | Often requires dedicated privacy chains |
| **Gas Efficiency** | Lower costs than ZK or FHE systems | High computational overhead |
| **Speed** | Lightweight crypto enables real-time mobile transactions | Heavy computation limits throughput |
| **Scalability** | Compatible with hardware acceleration | Limited by proof generation |
| **Compliance** | Selective disclosure for auditors and regulators | All-or-nothing privacy |
